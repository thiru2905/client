# Agent HR: Onboarding and Offboarding Workflow

## Onboarding Process

### Step 1: **Gather Employee Information**
- Collect the following details from the new employee:
    - Full Name
    - Date of Birth
    - Contact Information (Email, Phone)
    - Home Address
    - Emergency Contact Information
    - National ID (if applicable)
    - Bank Details (for payroll setup)
    - Social Security Number (SSN) or equivalent
    - Position/Role within the company
    - Team Name and Department
    - Name of Team Leader or Manager
    - Work Location (office, remote, hybrid)
    - Preferred Start Date

### Step 2: **Document Verification and Agreement**
- Send the following documents for signing:
    - Employment Contract
    - Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
    - Company Policies (Code of Conduct, Privacy Policy, etc.)
    - Benefits Enrollment Form
    - Tax Forms (W-4 or equivalent)
- Confirm that documents have been signed and stored securely.

### Step 3: **Assign Role, Team, and Access**
- Assign the new employee to their designated team.
- Set up their email address, system access, and any necessary software tools.
- Assign them a team leader or manager.
- Set up communication channels (Slack, Teams, etc.).

### Step 4: **Training and Orientation**
- Provide access to mandatory training materials, meeting notes / transcripts, meeting recordings and etc.
- Schedule an orientation session (in-person or virtual).
- Share the company’s culture, values, and mission with the new hire.
- Set expectations for the first 30/60/90 days.

### Step 5: **Employee Welcome**
- Send a personalized welcome email or message to the employee.
- Introduce the new hire to their team and schedule a meet-and-greet with the team leader.
- Provide access to internal employee resources (employee handbook, intranet, etc.).

### Step 6: **Onboarding Completion**
- Verify that the employee has received all necessary equipment (laptop, badge, etc.).
- Ensure that access to all systems and tools has been successfully granted.

## Offboarding Process

### Step 1: **Initiate Offboarding Request**
- Confirm the employee’s departure date.
- Notify HR and relevant managers about the offboarding process.
- Determine whether the departure is voluntary or involuntary.

### Step 2: **Exit Interview**
- Schedule an exit interview with the employee to gather feedback on their experience and reasons for leaving.
- Ask key questions such as:
    - What did you enjoy most about your role here?
    - What could we improve in terms of company culture, benefits, or support?
    - Would you recommend our company to others?
    - Do you have any suggestions for improving the workplace?
    - Is there anything we could have done to retain you?

### Step 3: **Asset Recovery**
- Retrieve company assets from the employee, such as:
    - Laptop or computer equipment
    - Company ID badge
    - Mobile phone or other devices
    - Keys or access cards
- Confirm that all company property is returned before finalizing offboarding.

### Step 4: **Access Revocation**
- Disable or transfer the employee’s access to all company systems, including:
    - Email accounts
    - Internal systems (CRM, HRIS, etc.)
    - External systems and software tools
    - Company VPN and network access
- Ensure that passwords, usernames, and any personal information are securely deleted.

### Step 5: **Final Pay and Benefits**
- Calculate the final paycheck, including:
    - Unused vacation days or PTO
    - Bonus, commission, or other outstanding payments
- Ensure benefits continuation or termination (e.g., health insurance, retirement plans).
- Provide the employee with necessary documentation regarding benefits after employment (e.g., COBRA in the U.S.).

### Step 6: **Offboarding Documentation**
- Generate and send the following documents to the employee:
    - Final paycheck details
    - Benefits status (continuation or termination)
    - Separation agreement or release (if applicable)
    - Certificate of Employment (if requested)
- Store all offboarding documents in the employee’s record.

### Step 7: **Notify Team & Update Records**
- Notify the employee’s team members and other relevant departments (IT, Facilities, etc.) of the employee’s departure.
- Update the employee’s status in the HR system and mark their profile as “Offboarded” or “Terminated.”
- Remove the employee from team communications (email lists, Slack channels, etc.).

### Step 8: **Exit Communication**
- Send a formal offboarding email confirming the employee’s last day, outlining next steps, and providing any last instructions.
- Send a “Thank You” message acknowledging the employee’s contributions to the company and wishing them well in their future endeavors.

### Step 9: **Offboarding Completion**
- Ensure that all offboarding steps have been completed before the employee's departure.
- Confirm that the employee is no longer in any company systems or communications.
- Finalize the offboarding process and close the employee’s record.
